⚗️ Core Mole Formulas

Moles from mass
n = mass (g) / Molar Mass (g/mol)
Mass from moles
mass = n × Molar Mass
Number of particles
N = n × 6.022 × 10²³
Moles from particles
n = N / 6.022 × 10²³

🔬 Gas Volume (STP)

Molar volume at STP
V = 22.4 L per mole
Moles from volume
n = Volume (L) / 22.4
Volume from moles
V = n × 22.4 L
STP conditions
0°C (273 K), 1 atm pressure

📊 % Composition & Formulas

% by mass of element
% = (mass of element / molar mass) × 100
Empirical formula step
Divide % by atomic mass → simplest ratio
Molecular formula
MF = n × EF where n = M / EF_mass

⚖️ Stoichiometry Method

Step 1
Balance the equation
Step 2
Convert given to moles (n = m/M)
Step 3
Use mole ratio from equation
Step 4
Convert moles back to grams/volume

🔑 Key Atomic Masses (NEET)

🎯 Limiting Reagent Method

Step 1
Find moles of each reactant
Step 2
Divide moles by stoichiometric coefficient
Step 3
Smaller value = Limiting Reagent
Step 4
Use LR moles × ratio to find product
